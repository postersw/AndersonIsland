Idie Ulsh Park


HIKING, SALMON CREEK

FACILITIES:
Fifteen deeply wooded acres; a short but very lush walk through some of the oldest cedars on the island.
  • 0.4 mile loop trail with moderate ups and downs starts on 108th St. just north of the old schoolhouse.
  • Schoolhouse creek, a salmon creek, runs by the eastern part of the trail. The creek continues under the road, onto Tom White park.
  • Portable toilet across the street at the Old Schoolhouse.
  • Dogs ARE allowed On-leash.

LOCATION:
Located between Otso Point Rd, 108th St., and Eckenstam-Johnson Road just north of the "Old School House".


HISTORY:
This park was opened in October 2018 and is dedicated to the memory of the Ulsh, Julin, and Petterson families. It was acquired with the support of "Ducks Unlimited" and "The North American Wetlands Conservation Council".
