child_care How to talk to children about the Coronavirus

  1. Avoid letting children listen to the news for more than shorts periods
  2. Discuss the news about the Coronavirus with honest and age-appropriate information. Assure them that your chance of acquiring the illness is very low.
  3. Focus on things your family can do to stay healthy and keep others healthy
  4. Teach them how to wash their hands properly – 20 seconds
  5. Talk about how to sneeze into a tissue or their elbow, and then wash their hands
  6. During difficult times, children do best if they have predictable routines and lots of time to play
  7. Talk with your children about others who are lonely who might benefit from your family reaching out to them in some way
  8. Remember that children will observe your behaviors and emotions for cues on how to manage their own feelings during this time

zoom_out_map How to protect yourself and your family from Coronavirus

  1. Wash your hands often with soap and water for at least 20 seconds
  2. Avoid touching your eyes, nose or mouth with unwashed hands
  3. Avoid close contact with sick people
  4. Use a tissue or your elbow to cover your mouth and nose when you sneeze or cough. Throw used tissues in the trash and wash your hands.
  5. Clean and disinfect frequently touched surfaces
  6. Stay home and away from others when you're sick

local_pharmacy Medical supplies to keep in stock but not hoard

  1. Soap; hand sanitizer is not needed if you wash your hands properly with soap and water.
  2. Acetaminophen (Tylenol) for fever or muscle aches. Consult your health care provider before taking Ibuprofen or other anti-inflammatories.
  3. Thermometer - your provider will need to know your temperature if you call about symptoms
  4. Masks are only for those who are sick - if you wear them, don't touch them. Please don’t hoard them.

local_phone Whom to call if you have symptoms of Coronavirus (fever, cough, shortness of breath)

  1. If you are having difficulty breathing or other serious health issues, call 9-1-1.
  2. Call your primary care provider for symptoms that aren’t life threatening. They will give you instructions about what to do.
  3. If you don’t have a primary care provider, call a Pierce County Coronavirus triage center:
    • Multicare - Free
    • CHI Fransiscan - Fee can be waived
    • Kaiser Permenente members - Call 1-800-297-6877 or 206-630-2244 (TTY 711) to talk to an Advice Nurse 24/7.
  4. Call an AI Health Ministry Nurse at 253 225-5173

local_hospital How to get to the hospital or a medical appointment if you are sick

  1. If you are having a medical emergency, call 9-1-1 and you will be transported to the hospital.
  2. If you are not having an emergency but are having flu/Coronavirus symptoms, call your primary care provider or one of the triage lines listed above for instructions.
  3. If you are instructed to go to the hospital, call 9-1-1 for transportation if you don’t have a family member to take you.

thumb_up How to decrease fear during this time

  1. Limit the amount of time you spend watching the news
  2. Do some creative activities
  3. Learn a new hobby (painting, knitting, carving, etc.)
  4. Write down some life stories, or write a letter in cursive to someone you love (a dying art)
  5. Cook or bake something unusual or new
  6. Have a new recipe potluck with neighbors
  7. Take photos around our island and post them

group Maintain connection with others -

  1. The mental health risks of social isolation, especially for the elderly is profound and often leads to depression, anxiety and suicidal thoughts.
  2. Call your neighbors, just to check in or to tell stories
  3. Look for others who have needs and help them in some way
  4. Have a movie contest (best, worst, most ridiculous, etc.)
  5. Offer a book club online
  6. Offer to teach someone a skill that you have
  7. Go for a walk/hike with a friend
  8. Get creative about staying connected

directions_run Increase your exercise

  1. Watch for activities offered by the gym that do not involve large groups
  2. Go for walks outside to get fresh air and enjoy our beautiful island

insert_emoticon Focus on the here and now.

Look for things that bring you joy, laughter or peace.

mood_bad If you are feeling overwhelmed by your circumstances

  1. Talk to friends and family about how you’re feeling
  2. Ask to be on the AI check-in list so you can be contacted every few days by a fellow islander. Call Jim Cook 253 858-8726 or Susanne Winkler 253 884-7737 or email Jim at jtcworld @ yahoo.com
  3. Seek professional help
  4. Call SAMHSA Disaster Distress Helpline at 1-800-985-5990 or text “TalkWithUS” to 66746
  5. Call National Suicide Prevention Lifeline at 1-800-273-TALK or text “TALK” to 741741
  6. Ask your Primary Care Provider where to get help
  7. Look online for mental health professionals
