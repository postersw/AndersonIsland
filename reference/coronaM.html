zoom_out_map How to protect yourself and your family from Coronavirus

  1. Wash your hands often with soap and water for at least 20 seconds
  2. Avoid touching your eyes, nose or mouth with unwashed hands
  3. Avoid close contact with sick people
  4. Use a tissue or your elbow to cover your mouth and nose when you sneeze or cough. Throw used tissues in the trash and wash your hands.
  5. Clean and disinfect frequently touched surfaces
  6. Stay home and away from others when you're sick

child_care How to talk to children about the Coronavirus

  1. Avoid letting children listen to the news for more than shorts periods
  2. Discuss the news about the Coronavirus with honest and age-appropriate information. Assure them that your chance of acquiring the illness is very low.
  3. Focus on things your family can do to stay healthy and keep others healthy
  4. Teach them how to wash their hands properly – 20 seconds
  5. Talk about how to sneeze into a tissue or their elbow, and then wash their hands
  6. During difficult times, children do best if they have predictable routines and lots of time to play
  7. Talk with your children about others who are lonely who might benefit from your family reaching out to them in some way
  8. Remember that children will observe your behaviors and emotions for cues on how to manage their own feelings during this time

local_phone Whom to call if you have symptoms of Coronavirus

  1. If you are having difficulty breathing or other serious health issues, call 9-1-1.
  2. Call your primary care provider for symptoms that aren’t life threatening. They will give you instructions about what to do.
  3. If you don’t have a primary care provider, call a Pierce County Coronavirus triage center:
    • Multicare - Free
    • CHI Franciscan - Fee can be waived
    • Kaiser Permenente members - Call 1-800-297-6877 or 206-630-2244 (TTY 711) to talk to an Advice Nurse 24/7.

  6. Write poems or document life stories

  8. Find interesting podcasts
  9. Binge on TED Talks
  10. Create homemade gifts
  11. Build a Lego creation and post it
  12. Cook or bake something new
  13. Take photos around the island and post them
  14. Do some bird watching

mood_bad If you are feeling overwhelmed

  1. Talk to friends and family about how you’re feeling
  2. Ask to be on the AI check-in list so you can be contacted every few days by a fellow islander. Call Jim Cook 253 858-8726 or Susanne Winkler 253 884-7737 or email Jim at jtcworld @ yahoo.com
  3. Seek professional help
    • Call SAMHSA Disaster Distress Helpline at 1-800-985-5990 or text “TalkWithUS” to 66746
    • Call National Suicide Prevention Lifeline at 1-800-273-TALK or text “TALK” to 741741
    • Ask your Primary Care Provider where to get help
    • Look online for mental health professionals
